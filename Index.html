The 35th Kabaka of Buganda & 1st Post Independence Leader of Uganda

Here's a few facts;

Sir Edward Frederick William David Walugembe Mutebi Luwangula Mutesa II (1924-1969) was the 35th Kabaka of the Kingdom of Buganda in Uganda. He reigned from 1939 until his death in 1969, playing a crucial role during Uganda's transition to independence.

Kabaka Mutesa II was educated at King's College Budo and later at Magdalene College, Cambridge. His reign witnessed significant political transformations including Uganda's independence from British colonial rule in 1962.

As the first President of Uganda (1963-1966), he worked to balance traditional authority with modern governance. His legacy continues to shape Ugandan politics and cultural identity.

He was the first African captain of the Cambridge rifle team

Avid Jazz Enthusiast & Band Leader: Mutesa II had a deep love for jazz music. He formed his own band, "The Mutesa II Band", which regularly performed at parties and gatherings at the Lubiri Palace. He was known to play the trumpet himself and actively encouraged modern music in Buganda.

"Mailo" Became a Symbolic Greeting: During the crisis surrounding his 1953 exile and the subsequent campaign for his return, the Baganda popularized the greeting "Mailo" (meaning "mile" but also a reference to the traditional land tenure system). It became a covert way to express solidarity and loyalty to the Kabaka without directly provoking colonial authorities.

Born at the Lubiri Palace in Mengo, Kampala to Kabaka Sir Daudi Chwa II and Lady Irene Drusilla Namaganda

1939

Became Kabaka of Buganda at age 15 following the death of his father

November 21, 1969

Died in London under mysterious circumstances

1971

His remains were returned to Uganda for a royal burial at Kasubi Tombs

Enduring Legacy

Kabaka Mutesa II's impact on modern Uganda

Political Influence

Kabaka Mutesa II played a pivotal role in Uganda's transition to independence. As both Kabaka of Buganda and first President of Uganda, he navigated complex relationships between traditional kingdoms and the new nation-state.

His book "Desecration of My Kingdom" details his perspective on the political turmoil that led to his exile.

Cultural Preservation

Mutesa II was a strong advocate for preserving Buganda's cultural heritage. During his reign, he:

  • Revived traditional ceremonies and rituals
  • Supported the documentation of oral histories
  • Promoted the teaching of Luganda language
  • Preserved royal sites including Kasubi Tombs

Modern Relevance

Today, Kabaka Mutesa II is remembered as a symbol of Buganda pride and Ugandan nationalism. His legacy continues through:

  • The annual Kabaka Birthday Run supporting health initiatives
  • Educational scholarships in his name
  • The Mutesa II Royal University
  • Ongoing cultural preservation efforts

Research & Scholarship

Academic resources on Kabaka Mutesa II

Our digital archive contains over 5,000 documents, photographs, and recordings related to Kabaka Mutesa II and his era. Researchers can access:

  • Original correspondence between Kabaka and colonial officials
  • Historical newspapers from the independence period
  • Oral history interviews with contemporaries
  • Academic papers on Buganda history
  • Digitized books from the royal collection

Contact our research department for access to special collections and research assistance.
